"Why is my dog licking his paw at 3 AM?"

I typed this at 2:14 AM on a Tuesday. Or maybe a Wednesday. Gus was asleep on the couch. He was not licking his paw. He had licked his paw earlier. For maybe 30 seconds. But at 2:14 AM, my brain decided this was a medical emergency. My brain is not trustworthy at 2:14 AM. My brain is barely trustworthy at 2:14 PM.

The Greatest Hits of My 2 AM Google History

"Can dogs eat grapes?"
Answer: No. Never. I knew this. I still googled it. Because Gus ate a grape that fell on the floor. One grape. I called the emergency vet. They said "one grape is probably fine for a 78-pound dog." I felt stupid. I still googled it again the next month.
"Dog ear infection smell"
Answer: Yeast smells like bread. Bacteria smells like corn chips. Gus's ears smelled like both. I panicked. The vet said "yeast." I had already imagined surgery. I had already priced ear surgery. It was $0. The yeast medication was $12.
"Golden retriever weight chart"
Answer: 65-75 pounds is ideal. Gus was 82. I found a chart that said 55-75. I found another that said 65-80. I found a third that said "depends on the individual." I wanted a number. I got three numbers and a philosophy.
"Cat screaming at night normal?"
Answer: Sometimes. But also maybe not. Mochi screamed for a week when I stopped free-feeding. The internet said "she's hungry." The internet was right. The internet is sometimes right. Not often. But sometimes.
"Why does my dog eat grass?"
Answer: No one knows. Seriously. Scientists have studied this. They don't know. Gus eats grass. Then he vomits. Then he eats more grass. It's a cycle. It's a grass-vomit-grass cycle. The internet has theories. The theories are all wrong. No one knows.
"Is my dog dreaming or having a seizure?"
Answer: If the paws are twitching and he's making little noises, it's a dream. If the whole body is rigid and the eyes are open, it's a seizure. I have checked Gus's eyes at 3 AM. They were closed. It was a dream. About squirrels. Probably.

The 2 AM spiral is a ritual. It's not rational. It's not helpful. But it's universal. Every pet owner I know has done it. My neighbor with Waffles the beagle googled "beagle ate chocolate" at 1 AM. It was a chocolate chip. One chip. Waffles weighs 32 pounds. The chocolate calculator said "probably fine." My neighbor did not sleep. He watched Waffles all night. Waffles slept fine. Waffles is a beagle. Beagles sleep through anything.

I built PetCalCentral because of the 2 AM spiral. Because I was tired of googling "dog calorie calculator" and finding sites that wanted my email address. Because I was tired of finding forums where half the people said "feed raw" and half said "feed kibble" and everyone was angry. Because I wanted numbers. I wanted exact numbers. I wanted to know that Gus needs 1,650 calories per day, not "about 2 cups." I wanted to stop guessing. I wanted to stop spiraling.

The calculators don't stop the spiraling entirely. I still google things. I still panic. But now I have a starting point. Now I have a number to take to the vet. Now I have a spreadsheet to update instead of a search history to regret. The spreadsheet is better. The spreadsheet doesn't judge me. The spreadsheet doesn't say "have you tried essential oils." The spreadsheet just shows numbers. Numbers are better than forums. Numbers are better than 2 AM panic.

Gus is asleep right now. It's 10 PM. He's not licking his paw. He's snoring. Mochi is on the desk. She's knocking things off. She's about to knock my pen off. There it goes. The pen is on the floor. Mochi is satisfied. She will sleep now. I will not google anything tonight. I will sleep. I will probably not sleep. I will probably check the spreadsheet one more time. Row 26. Weight: 78.0. Status: fine. Everything is fine.

The 2 AM spiral is part of pet ownership. It's part of love. It's part of caring so much that your brain invents emergencies at midnight. It's not rational. But it's real. And if you're reading this at 2 AM, googling something about your pet, you're not alone. We're all here. We're all spiraling. And we're all going to be fine. Probably. Call your vet in the morning. Not the emergency vet. Just your regular vet. They'll tell you it's fine. And it will be fine. And you'll sleep eventually. Maybe.
