The Real Way to Calculate Dog Years (Not ×7)

May 14, 2026 · 3 min read

"Multiply by 7." That's what everyone says. Your dog is 5? That means they're 35 in human years. Easy, right?

Wrong. That rule is complete nonsense that someone made up decades ago and we just... kept repeating. Like a game of telephone, but for dog math.

Why the ×7 Rule is Wrong

Dogs age differently than humans. A lot faster when they're young, then slower as they get older. Think about it:

  • • A 1-year-old dog can have puppies. A 7-year-old human cannot.
  • • Dogs reach sexual maturity at 6-12 months. Humans take 12+ years.
  • • But small dogs live 15+ years, while humans don't stop at 105.

Fun fact: In 2019, researchers at UC San Diego published a formula based on DNA methylation. It goes like this:

Human age = 16 × ln(dog age) + 31

Yeah, there's logarithms. Sorry.

Size Matters Too

Here's another thing nobody tells you: size affects how fast dogs age.

  • • Small dogs (under 20 lbs): Age slower, live longer
  • • Medium dogs (20-50 lbs): Middle of the road
  • • Large dogs (50+ lbs): Age faster, shorter lifespan

A Great Dane at 7 is basically a senior citizen. A chihuahua at 7 is still a somewhat energetic menace.

The Bottom Line

Forget the ×7 rule. It's misleading and makes you either under- or over-estimate your dog's age.

Use a calculator that actually accounts for size and breed. Your dog deserves accurate math.
