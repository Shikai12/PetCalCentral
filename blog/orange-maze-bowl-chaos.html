I Bought a Slow Feeder Bowl. Here's What Actually Happened.

Gus puked on the rug again. Third time this month. I ordered an orange maze bowl at midnight. Here's the honest review.

Gus puked on the rug again. Third time this month. Always the same rug. The one I actually like. The one that cost $180 from West Elm. I was standing there at 11:47 PM, holding a roll of paper towels and a bottle of enzyme cleaner, wondering what I was doing with my life. My dog was eating too fast. That's it. That's the whole problem. But it was 11:47 PM and I was angry at a rug and I opened Amazon.

I searched "slow feeder bowl for large dogs." I didn't read reviews. I looked at pictures. I found an orange maze bowl that looked like it would take Gus at least five minutes to figure out. I clicked buy. It arrived Thursday. Or maybe Friday. The days all look the same lately.

The first feeding was chaos. Gus stared at the bowl. He pawed at it. He flipped it over. The kibble went everywhere. He ate the kibble off the floor in 30 seconds. The bowl sat there, empty, upside down, mocking me. I flipped it back. I refilled it. He flipped it again. We did this three times. I was sweating. It was 7 AM. I hadn't had coffee.

By day three, something shifted. Gus stopped flipping the bowl. I don't know why. Maybe he got tired. Maybe he realized the floor kibble was the same as the bowl kibble. Maybe he developed a sense of dignity. Probably not that last one. He started using his tongue to navigate the maze. It took him 8 minutes to finish his meal. Eight minutes. Previously it was 45 seconds. I timed it. I have a spreadsheet.

The vomiting stopped. Completely. No more rug incidents. The enzyme cleaner sits unused under the sink. I should probably throw it out. But I keep it. Just in case. Just in case Gus finds a new way to destroy my home.

I bought a second slow feeder bowl for my office. I work from home. Gus eats lunch at 1 PM while I'm on a Zoom call. My coworkers think I have a water feature. It's just Gus licking an orange maze bowl for 12 minutes. I don't correct them. It's better than explaining that my dog used to vomit on my rug because he has no self-control.

The bowl has a flaw. A design flaw. Gus learned that if he picks it up with his mouth and drops it from a height of approximately 6 inches, the kibble dislodges from the maze and scatters across the floor. He then eats the floor kibble in 20 seconds. I measured the drop height. It's consistent. He's not a genius. He's just persistent. And hungry. Mostly hungry.

I solved this by placing the bowl inside a larger, heavier ceramic bowl. The ceramic bowl is too heavy to flip. The slow feeder sits inside it like a Russian nesting doll of pet feeding anxiety. Gus now takes 10-12 minutes to eat. He still tries to flip the ceramic bowl. He cannot. He accepts this. He licks the maze. He is defeated but fed. I am victorious but tired.

Dr. Martinez said slow feeders are recommended for dogs who eat too fast. She said it reduces bloat risk. I didn't know about bloat. I just knew about the rug. Now I know about bloat. Great. Another thing to worry about at 2 AM. But the bowl helps. The bowl genuinely helps. Gus is calmer after meals. He doesn't do the post-dinner pacing anymore. He just lies down and dreams about tater tots. I can hear him dreaming. He makes little chewing noises.

I spent $24.99 on the orange maze bowl. I spent $12.99 on the ceramic bowl. I spent $180 on the rug. I spent $0 on my sanity. The slow feeder was worth it. The rug is still clean. The enzyme cleaner is still under the sink. Gus is still hungry. But he's slower now. And that's something.
